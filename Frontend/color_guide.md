To build a brand that feels **futuristic, sleek, and minimalist** in 2026, you need to move beyond standard "tech blue" and embrace **Atmospheric UI** and **Bioluminescent Accents**.

This guide combines the high-performance logic of your backend with the latest design trends: **Bento Grids**, **Glassmorphism 2.0**, and **OLED-optimized depth**.

## **1. The Brand DNA: "Bioluminescent Tech"**

In 2026, the trend is moving away from flat colors toward **Dynamic Gradients** that feel like light sources. Your primary brand identity should use a "Hyper-Violet" to "Cyber-Cyan" mesh.

| Role | Color Name | Hex Code | Vibe |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary** | **Electric Indigo** | `#6366F1` | The "AI Magic" color. |
| **Accent** | **Cyber Cyan** | `#06B6D4` | Speed, technology, and energy. |
| **High-Lite** | **Neon Lime** | `#BEF264` | Viral growth and "Success" states. |

**The 2026 Trend:** Use these as a **Mesh Gradient** for your landing page hero and as a **Border Beam** (a thin, glowing 1px line that travels around cards) in your dashboard.

---

## **2. Dark Mode: The "Bioluminescent Void"**

This is your primary workspace. Instead of pure black, use **Deep Slate/Teal** to give the UI a sense of infinite depth.

* **Background:** `#09090B` (Rich Zinc Black)
* **Surface (Cards/Bento Boxes):** `#121217` (Deep Charcoal with 2% Blue tint)
* **Border:** `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.08)` (Ultra-thin, almost invisible)
* **Active Agent Glow:** `rgba(99, 102, 241, 0.15)` (Soft Indigo outer glow)

---

## **3. Light Mode: The "Cloud Dancer" Studio**

Based on the **2026 Pantone trend (Cloud Dancer)**, your Light Mode should feel airy, expensive, and clean. It avoids "hospital white" for a softer, organic off-white.

* **Background:** `#F2F4F7` (Cloud Dancer / Soft Pearl)
* **Surface:** `#FFFFFF` (Pure White)
* **Border:** `#E2E8F0` (Light Slate)
* **Primary Text:** `#0F172A` (Ink Navy — easier on the eyes than pure black)

---

## **4. The "Glass & Glow" System (Special Effects)**

To achieve that **"Sleek & Futuristic"** look, use these CSS-inspired effects for your 5-agent pipeline:

### **Glassmorphism 2.0**

For your "HITL" (Human-in-the-Loop) popups, use a frosted glass effect. This keeps the user focused while maintaining the context of the workflow behind it.

* **Blur:** `20px`
* **Opacity:** `70%`
* **Border:** `1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.1)`

### **The "Bento Box" Layout**

Organize your agents into a **Bento Grid**. Each agent gets its own "box" with varying sizes:

* **Big Box:** The Video Preview (Veo 3.1 output).
* **Medium Box:** The Script Editor (Gemini 2.5 Pro).
* **Small Boxes:** Token usage, Research images, and Style selectors.

---

## **5. Typography: The "Precision" Set**

Minimalism relies on typography. Use a **Variable Font** to switch weights dynamically between headers and body.

* **Primary Font:** **Geist** or **Inter Tight**. These are high-readability sans-serifs that look incredibly "engineered."
* **Monospace (For Metadata):** **JetBrains Mono**. Use this for the "Technical Logs" of what the agents are doing (e.g., *"Agent 2: Fetching Google Search data..."*).
