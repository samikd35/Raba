A **value proposition statement** is a clear sentence (or two) that explains **why a specific customer should choose your product or service instead of alternatives.**

It answers four core questions in one place:

1. **Who is it for?**
   Your **target customer** or segment.

2. **What problem do you solve?**
   The **pain**, challenge, or need you’re addressing.

3. **What do they get?**
   The **specific benefit or outcome** (save time, make money, reduce risk, feel better, etc.).

4. **Why you, not others?**
   Your **differentiation** — what makes you better or unique (faster, cheaper, easier, more accurate, more trusted, etc.).

---

### A simple structure you can use

You don’t have to follow this exactly, but this template is very helpful:

> **For** [target customer]
> **who** [key problem / need],
> **[product/service name]** is a [category]
> that **[main benefit / outcome]**.
> **Unlike** [main alternative], it **[key differentiator].**

Example:

> For early-stage founders in Africa
> who struggle to validate business ideas quickly,
> Yuba is an AI-powered venture building platform
> that helps them test, refine, and validate ideas in days instead of months.
> Unlike traditional incubators, it’s always available online and personalized to each founder.

---

### What a good value proposition statement should be

* **Clear, not fancy** – easy enough for a 15-year-old to understand.
* **Specific** – avoids vague words like “innovative” or “cutting-edge” without explanation.
* **Outcome-focused** – talks about results (e.g., “reduce patient record errors”) not just features.
* **Credible** – sounds realistic; you can back it up with your product or traction.

---

### How it’s different from other things

* **Not a slogan** (e.g., “Just Do It”)
* **Not a mission statement** (e.g., “We aim to transform healthcare in Africa”)
* It’s a **practical, focused message** used for your pitch, website hero section, decks, and sales conversations to quickly explain *why you’re valuable* to a specific user.
